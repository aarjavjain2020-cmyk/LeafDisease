How this scan works

Every pixel is converted from red-green-blue values into hue, saturation, value — the same color space OpenCV's cv2.cvtColor uses. Pixels in the healthy green hue band count as leaf tissue; pixels in the yellow-brown band, or very dark or grayish pixels, count as lesion tissue. A connected-components pass (like cv2.findContours) groups lesion pixels into blobs — their count, size, and whether they touch the leaf edge suggest a pattern, not a lab diagnosis. No model, no internet connection — everything runs on this device.

Why no exact disease name: color and shape alone can't reliably tell a fungal blight from a nutrient deficiency — many look alike to a color filter. That's what Stage 2's trained neural network will add.
